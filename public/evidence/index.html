2. Formal Letter & Outcome Writing Structure

Employee Relations roles require clear, legally safer correspondence. This structure can be adapted to disciplinary, capability, absence or probation meetings.

Meeting invite structure

  • Opening: Confirm the purpose of the meeting and the process being followed.
  • Issue summary: Set out the concerns clearly using dates, examples and evidence references.
  • Potential outcome: Explain the possible range of outcomes where appropriate, without prejudging the decision.
  • Right to be accompanied: Confirm the right to be accompanied by a work colleague or trade union representative where applicable.
  • Evidence: List documents attached or available for review.
  • Adjustments: Invite the employee to request any reasonable adjustments or support needed to attend.
  • Next steps: Confirm date, time, chairperson and how to submit evidence or questions.

Outcome letter quality checklist

CheckGood practice
Neutral toneAvoid emotional, accusatory or vague wording.
Evidence trailReference what was considered and what weight was given.
Employee responseRecord the employee's key explanation and mitigation.
Decision rationaleExplain why the outcome is reasonable and proportionate.
ExpectationsSet measurable actions, standards, support and review dates.
AppealClearly explain appeal route, deadline and recipient.

3. Absence, Welfare & Probation Support

This section shows how managers can be supported to handle absence, welfare and probation concerns early and consistently.

Welfare meeting checklist

  • Confirm the purpose is support, not punishment.
  • Discuss current health, absence reason, likely return, support needed and any medical advice.
  • Consider reasonable adjustments, phased return, temporary duties or Occupational Health referral where appropriate.
  • Avoid pressure to disclose excessive medical information; focus on work impact and support.
  • Document agreed actions, review dates and contact arrangements.

Probation review framework

6. Manager Advisory Playbook

This one-page playbook shows practical, commercial and legally safer advice HR can give managers when people issues arise.

SituationManager should avoidRecommended HR-guided action
Employee performance concernDelaying feedback until frustration builds.Hold early informal conversation, give examples, agree support and review date.
Conduct concernMaking assumptions or using emotional language.Record facts, preserve evidence, speak to HR and decide whether informal or formal action is proportionate.
Sickness absence patternTreating all absence as misconduct.Hold RTW/welfare discussion, explore support, review triggers and consider medical advice where needed.
Grievance raisedDefending the business immediately.Acknowledge concern, clarify issues, appoint suitable investigator and keep records.
Probation issuePassing probation because the deadline was missed.Review evidence before deadline, document concerns and extend or fail only with clear rationale and process.

What this proves

  • Ability to coach managers without overcomplicating advice.
  • Commercial judgement: resolve early where possible, escalate where necessary.
  • Fair process awareness and clear documentation habits.
  • Practical readiness for Employee Relations Specialist roles.
