1. ER Case Study - Additional Employment and Working Pattern Concern

What I did: I supported the structuring of an investigation where Employee X was working regular shifts while also appearing to hold substantial external employment. I helped organise the facts, clarify the allegations, and prepare management for an evidence-led decision.

Business challenge

The organisation needed to establish whether there had been a failure to declare additional employment, whether working patterns created fatigue or safe-working risk, and whether managers had followed the right controls when approving regular shifts.

Actions I completed

  • Built a clear chronology using rota records, clock-in/out reports, payroll entries and employee declaration records.
  • Separated the concern into specific investigation headings: additional employment declaration, availability accuracy, safe working, manager approval and policy compliance.
  • Prepared neutral wording for an investigation invite so the employee had a fair opportunity to explain before any decision was made.
  • Identified the wider process gap: additional employment declarations needed to be clearer and checked regularly for both bank and contracted staff.

Result

  • Management received a clearer evidence trail before deciding the next formal step.
  • The case was handled more fairly because concerns were framed as questions to investigate, not conclusions.
  • The organisation gained a practical improvement point: strengthen additional employment declarations and review triggers.

2. ER Case Study - Allegation Against a Manager

What I did: I supported the handling of an employee concern involving alleged inappropriate management communication, unfair treatment and a breakdown in working relationship.

Business challenge

The complaint needed to be investigated fairly without automatically defending the manager or assuming wrongdoing. The key was to separate facts, perception, management style and any evidence of unfair treatment.

Actions I completed

  • Converted a broad complaint into specific investigation points that could be answered with evidence.
  • Prepared a timeline using meeting notes, rota decisions, messages and management responses.
  • Helped identify whether the matter required informal resolution, grievance investigation, management coaching or formal escalation.
  • Used neutral language so both the employee and manager had a fair chance to respond.

3. Compliance Case Study - CQC and Local Authority Audit Readiness

Business challenge

Evidence was spread across different records and managers needed a clear view of what was complete, what was missing and what needed urgent follow-up before audit review.

Actions I completed

  • Reviewed staff file evidence against recruitment, onboarding, DBS, right to work, training and supervision requirements.
  • Maintained trackers showing missing items, responsible person, due date and completion status.
  • Chased managers and administrators for outstanding evidence and clarified what documents were acceptable.
  • Grouped gaps into quick fixes, manager follow-ups and higher-risk compliance actions.

4. Process Improvement Case Study - Additional Employment Declaration Control

What I built: I created a clearer additional employment declaration approach to help managers identify external work, availability issues and fatigue risk earlier.

Business challenge

Managers may not always know when staff have other employment or agency work. This can create rota, fatigue, availability, conflict of interest and compliance risk.

Actions I completed

  • Created clearer declaration questions covering other employment, agency work, self-employment and changes in circumstances.
  • Added prompts for employer type, role, estimated weekly hours and whether outside work could affect safe performance.
  • Introduced HR review triggers where working hours, fatigue, availability or compliance concerns may apply.
  • Linked the declaration to onboarding and annual declaration chasing.

5. Documentation Case Study - Investigation Outcome Structure

What I drafted: I created a structured investigation outcome format to help managers explain findings without prejudging disciplinary decisions.

Business challenge

Investigation outcomes can become risky if they sound like a final decision has already been made. The wording needs to show what was reviewed, what was found and why the next step is reasonable.

Actions I completed

  • Separated investigation findings from disciplinary decision-making.
  • Used clear headings: issues investigated, evidence reviewed, employee response, findings and next step.
  • Kept the tone factual, balanced and proportionate.
  • Included wording confirming that any formal hearing would make the final decision after considering the employee response.

Sample wording

Following the investigation, there appears to be sufficient evidence to proceed to a formal hearing in relation to the accuracy of additional employment and availability information. This does not mean that a decision has been made. A hearing manager will consider the evidence and your response before deciding the appropriate outcome.

Result

  • Improved consistency of ER correspondence.
  • Reduced the risk of prejudged or emotionally worded outcomes.
  • Helped managers understand the difference between investigation findings and formal decisions.
