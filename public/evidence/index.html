1. Sample ER Investigation - Secondary Employment During Maternity Leave

Scenario: Employee X is engaged on a bank contract with Organisation A but has regularly accepted full-time shifts. During a period recorded as maternity leave with another employer, a concern is raised that Employee X may also have worked full-time shifts at Organisation A while holding a full-time NHS role. This sample demonstrates how HR would structure an investigation into secondary employment, working time, fatigue risk, declaration accuracy and policy compliance.

Allegations to investigate

  • Employee X may have failed to declare secondary employment or changes in working arrangements.
  • Employee X may have worked hours that created fatigue, safe staffing, working time or health and safety risk.
  • Employee X may have provided inaccurate information about availability, maternity status or employment commitments.
  • There may be payroll, rota, right to work, visa/sponsorship or contractual implications depending on the employee status and documents held.

HR judgement: Do not assume dishonesty before investigation. The issue should be framed as a fact-finding process, giving Employee X the opportunity to explain contract type, maternity dates, NHS role, availability, declarations and any misunderstanding.

Evidence areaDocuments/review actionReason for review
Contract and statusCheck bank contract, signed terms, staff handbook, secondary employment policy and maternity/availability documents.Clarifies whether obligations applied and whether declaration rules were clear.
Rota and attendanceReview shifts accepted, clock-in/out reports, rota records and manager approvals for the relevant period.Confirms actual working pattern and whether hours were full-time in practice.
DeclarationsReview annual declaration, additional employment declaration, RTW forms and any changes submitted by Employee X.Tests whether the employee disclosed other employment and whether records were current.
Payroll and leave recordsCompare paid shifts, absence/maternity-related records and any internal leave notes.Identifies inconsistencies without making assumptions.
Manager evidenceInterview rota manager, home manager/admin and payroll contact where relevant.Establishes who knew what, when and what was approved.
Employee responseInvite Employee X to investigation meeting with evidence summary and allow written/meeting response.Ensures fair process and right of reply.

Sample investigation invite wording

Dear Employee X, I am writing to invite you to an investigation meeting as part of a fact-finding process. The purpose of the meeting is to understand concerns relating to your working arrangements, including whether secondary employment was declared, the number of shifts accepted, and whether there were any working time, fatigue, policy or record accuracy concerns. No decision has been made at this stage. You will have the opportunity to provide your explanation and any documents you wish to be considered.

Possible outcomes depending on evidence

  • No further action if evidence shows disclosure was made, hours were safe and records were accurate.
  • Informal guidance or record correction where misunderstanding is minor and risk is low.
  • Formal disciplinary process where evidence suggests failure to disclose, dishonesty, unsafe working or serious policy breach.
  • Process improvement action if managers accepted repeated full-time bank work without appropriate declaration checks.

2. Sample Investigation - Allegation Against Manager

Scenario: Employee Y raises concerns that Manager Z spoke aggressively, failed to address a workplace concern fairly and treated them differently after they challenged rota allocation. This sample shows how HR can separate grievance, conduct, management style and retaliation concerns.

StepHR actionPurpose
Acknowledge concernConfirm receipt, clarify whether the employee is raising a formal grievance or seeking informal resolution.Avoid misclassifying the issue and confirm expectations.
Risk triageCheck whether there is safeguarding, bullying, whistleblowing, discrimination, retaliation or health concern.Escalate immediately where risk is high.
Appoint investigatorSelect an impartial manager not directly involved.Supports independence and fairness.
Gather evidenceReview emails, rota decisions, meeting notes, witness accounts and previous concerns.Builds factual timeline.
Interview managerPut specific concerns to Manager Z neutrally and allow response.Ensures balanced investigation.
Outcome reportSet out findings, evidence relied upon, unresolved points and recommendations.Creates defensible documentation.

Sample neutral allegation wording

It is alleged that on or around [date], Manager Z communicated with Employee Y in a manner that may not have met expected management standards. It is also alleged that subsequent rota decisions may have been influenced by Employee Y raising concerns. The investigation will consider whether policy expectations were met and whether any management action, support or formal process is required.

HR judgement: Even where allegations are not upheld, HR may still recommend management coaching, clearer communication standards, improved note-taking or mediation if working relationships have been damaged.

3. CQC / Local Authority Audit Readiness Sample

This sample shows how HR evidence can be organised before an external or internal compliance review. It is designed for regulated care settings but is transferable to any high-compliance people environment.

Audit areaEvidence expectedHR action / quality check
Recruitment fileApplication, interview notes, references, DBS, RTW, offer, contract and role profile.Check completeness and consistency before audit.
TrainingMandatory training matrix, induction, role-specific training and refreshers.Identify gaps with action owner and target date.
Supervision and probationInduction reviews, probation outcomes, supervisions and appraisals.Confirm manager oversight and timely review.
Absence and welfareRTW forms, welfare meeting notes and support plans where applicable.Ensure records are proportionate and securely stored.
ER recordsCase chronology, invite letters, meeting notes, outcome letters and appeal documents.Ensure process is fair, clear and evidence-based.
Policy acknowledgementSigned handbook/policy declarations and annual declarations.Confirm staff understand key obligations including additional employment.

Sample audit summary wording

The HR review identified strong evidence control in right to work, DBS and contract records. Areas requiring follow-up were training refresher dates, inconsistent supervision evidence and incomplete annual declaration returns. Managers were provided with an action tracker showing missing items, owner, due date and completion status.

4. Sample Additional Employment Declaration Control

This sample links directly to the secondary employment scenario. It shows a practical control that could prevent similar issues from becoming bigger ER problems.

QuestionExpected responseHR review trigger
Do you currently work for another employer or agency?Yes/No, employer name, role and average hours.If yes, review working time, fatigue and conflict of interest risk.
Has this changed since your last declaration?Yes/No with details.If yes, update employee file and notify manager/payroll if needed.
Could your other work affect safe performance of this role?Employee explanation.Escalate where fatigue, availability or safeguarding risk appears.
Are you subject to visa or sponsorship conditions?Yes/No with HR verification, not public display.Check working hour/role restrictions where applicable.
Employee confirmationSigned declaration that information is accurate and changes will be reported.Failure to disclose may become conduct issue.

Policy wording sample

Employees must declare any secondary employment, agency work, self-employment or material change in working arrangements. This helps the organisation manage working time, fatigue, conflicts of interest, safe staffing and compliance obligations. Failure to provide accurate information may be considered under the disciplinary procedure.

5. Sample Outcome Letter Structure - Investigation Only

This is not a disciplinary outcome. It is a structure for closing the investigation stage and deciding whether no further action, informal action or formal hearing is required.

SectionContent to includeQuality standard
OpeningConfirm investigation has concluded and explain purpose of letter.Clear and neutral.
Issues investigatedList each allegation/concern separately.Specific, not vague.
Evidence reviewedSummarise documents, interviews and employee response.Balanced and proportionate.
FindingsState whether there is sufficient evidence to proceed, not final guilt.Avoid prejudging disciplinary outcome.
Next stepNo further action, informal guidance, training/process action or formal hearing.Proportionate to evidence and risk.
SupportSignpost welfare support, right to respond and next process steps.Human and legally safer.

Sample wording

Following the investigation, there is sufficient evidence to proceed to a formal disciplinary hearing in relation to the declaration of secondary employment and the accuracy of availability information. This does not mean a decision has been made. The hearing manager will consider the evidence and your response before deciding the appropriate outcome.
